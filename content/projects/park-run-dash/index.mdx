## The constraint

Build a playable game with nothing but a canvas element, vanilla
JavaScript, and CSS. No engine, no framework, no build step. Three files.

Park Run Dash is a side-scroller themed on the Saturday-morning 5k:
you run, you dodge, you try to hold pace to the finish line.

## What it actually took

The game loop was the easy part. The real work — the part that doesn't
show up in a screenshot — was making it feel right on phones:

- **Input** — touch controls with tap fallbacks, because "works on my
  desktop keyboard" is not a control scheme.
- **Safari** — landscape fullscreen on iOS Safari has its own opinions
  about viewport units, address bars, and orientation. Winning that fight
  took more commits than the gameplay did.
- **Small screens** — maximising the playfield in landscape without
  clipping the HUD, across an unreasonable range of aspect ratios.

## Lessons

Constraints are clarifying. With no engine to lean on, every frame drawn
is a decision you made. And shipping something small but *finished* —
playable by anyone, on whatever device is in their pocket — teaches you
more about the platform than a tutorial ever will.
